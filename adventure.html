Adventure tour

Water adventures

Kayaking in lagoon (Iceland)

These sit-on-top kayaks are extremely easy to steer, and no previous kayaking experience is required. Once on the kayak and you push off into the lagoon, you'll be immediately immersed into a world of iceberg-filled calm waters, with breathtaking views of the Sólheimajökull Glacier, which is only able to be seen while on water. After a few hours of gliding along the stillness of the lagoon, you will head back to shore having collected some unforgettable memories.

Rafting in Bovec (Slovenia)

Cruise down the crystal clear waters of the Soca River on this white-water rafting tour from Bovec. Change into your rafting equipment and listen to a safety briefing given by your certified guide, who will tell you everything you need to know. Embrace the rush of paddling over the rapids and watch it back later with a video of the experience.

Canyoning waterfalls (Slovenia)

Discover the fun of canyoning in the clear waters of Savinja River in Upper Savinja Valley. During canyoning in Slovenia, you will get to know Cuc Canyon and its stunning waterfalls, which are located on the route of the tour.

Snorkeling with dolphins (Portugal)

Board your boat and head towards the area with a 99% chance of seeing dolphins or whales. Get the opportunity to be in the water with one of the two species of dolphins Madeira Island regulations allow: common dolphins or spotted dolphins. As you enter the water, remain safely connected to the boat at all times. Get comfortable in the water and approach the dolphins with the support of your guide. Once you're ready, get in the water with the dolphins in front, below, and next to you and admire these beautiful mammals up close, and in their natural habitat.
Hiking tours

Hiking in Tatras (Slovakia)

Tatra Mountains uncover 85 mountain lakes, of which Velke Hincovo pleso (20 ha, 53 m deep) is the largest and the deepest. The Tatra region is a frequent target of visitors worldwide for its unique beauty and many possibilities for hiking. The most popular hiking tours are hut-to-hut style but day hikes from one hotel are equally adventurous while offering more flexibility.

Trolltunga hike (Norway)

Trolltunga is one of Norway's most famous "icons" and is located 1100 meters above Lake Ringedalsvatnet. On the hike to the top, you will be surrounded by stunning nature, safely led by your experienced and knowledgeable guide. Safely at the top, you are rewarded with an unbeatable view of the Ringedal lake and the Folgefonna glacier. Plus, standing on the edge should add some butterflies to your tummy. It is not without reason that Trolltunga is one of the most photographed icons in the world and that spectacular photos from the top can be found on countless Instagram accounts.

Saxon National Park (Germany)

Saxon Switzerland National Park is a magnificent nature reserve that suffers from mistaken identity. You'd be forgiven if you thought it was in Switzerland, but you'll actually find this forested, rocky wonderland in eastern Germany on the border with the Czech Republic. Saxon Switzerland got its name from two Swiss men who explored the area in the 18th century and were reminded of their home country. Although established in 1990, the park's 36 square miles of land still feel relatively undiscovered by tourists — which is just one of many reasons why Saxon Switzerland is a hiker' dream.

Hiking along Schäfler Ridge (Switzerland)

he Schäfler ridge hike is not just one of the best routes in the Appenzell region, but in all of Switzerland. Its peak sits at an altitude of 1925 meters, and whilst it's not the tallest peak in the area, it's one of the most popular due to the demanding snake-like trail that cascades around the rugged mountain. There are many routes to the summit, however, the route I'm going to cover is, in my opinion, one of the best. This is because it not only includes Schäfler, but also Ebenalp and Seealpsee, which are two areas of this mountain range that are also worth visiting.
Aerial entertainment

Swiss Alps helicopter tour (Switzerland)

It can be tricky to appreciate the sheer scale of the Swiss Alps on a ground tour. Take the skies on helicopter tour and discover sweeping panoramas of the Eiger, Monch, and Jungfrau peaks while learning more about the region from the professional pilot. Ideal for a honeymoon or small family group, this intimate sightseeing trip makes your Alpine adventure all the more memorable.

Rioja Wine from above (Spain)

xperience Spain's Rioja Wine region from above on this hot air balloon adventure. After meeting up with a guide at a designated location, enjoy convenient transportation to the departure point. Then, lift off and marvel at vineyards, mountains, and the Ebro River. Enjoy a complimentary toast with a locally produced Cava, a flight diploma, and, after landing, a delicious, authentic breakfast.

Tandem skydiving in Prague (Czech)

Take to the Czech skies on a tandem skydiving excursion from Prague. After a detailed briefing, put on your provided goggles, harness, and suit. Once the plane reaches 15,000 feet, it's time to jump. Feel secure attached to your guide and enjoy a 60 second free fall, followed by a five to seven minute parachute ride. Leave with a certificate and t-shirt to commemorate your adrenaline fueled adventure.
